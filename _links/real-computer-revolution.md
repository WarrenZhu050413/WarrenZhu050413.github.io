"One of the realizations we had about computers in the 60s was that they give rise to new and more powerful forms of arguments about many important issues via _dynamic simulation_. That is, instead of making the fairly dry claims that can be stated in prose and mathematical equations, the computer _could carry out the implications of the claims_ to provide a better sense of whether the claims constituted a worthwhile model of reality.""
