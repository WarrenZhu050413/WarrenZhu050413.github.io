Visual Influences

Artists to study for experimental photography

Photographers

Layering & Double Exposure

  • Eikoh Hosoe — Kamaitachi series, Mishima collaboration
  • Antoine d'Agata — red washes, bodies in extremis
  • Francesca Woodman — figures dissolving into architecture
  • Jerry Uelsmann — master of analog photomontage
  • Man Ray — rayographs, solarization
  • Ralph Eugene Meatyard — masks, suburban dread

Noir & Psychological Weight

  • Bill Henson — shadow-heavy, velvety darkness
  • Anton Corbijn — gritty monochrome portraiture
  • Daidō Moriyama — high-contrast, anxiety as texture

Painters & Visual Artists

Flesh, Tension, Confinement

  • Francis Bacon — smeared flesh, panic, small triptych panels
  • Berlinde De Bruyckere — wax sculptures, vulnerability of skin
  • Zdzisław Beksiński — nightmare atmosphere, organic abstraction
  • Egon Schiele — contorted figures, erotic unease
  • Goya (Black Paintings) — dread without gothic clichés

Horror & Surreal

  • H.R. Giger — biomechanical sensual horror
  • Dave McKean — collage/photography/paint hybrids

Filmmakers

Body Under Duress

  • David Cronenberg — Videodrome, Dead Ringers
  • Shinya Tsukamoto — Tetsuo, Vital, Kotoko
  • Andrzej Żuławski — Possession
  • Philippe Grandrieux — Sombre, La Vie Nouvelle (closest match)

Color as Violence

  • Dario Argento — Suspiria, Inferno
  • Gaspar Noé — Irreversible, Climax, Enter the Void
  • Nicolas Winding Refn — The Neon Demon

Atmosphere Over Narrative

  • David Lynch — Eraserhead, Inland Empire
  • Kiyoshi Kurosawa — Cure, Kairo
  • Pier Paolo Pasolini — Salò

Writers

Body as Rupture

  • Georges Bataille — Story of the Eye, Erotism
  • Yukio Mishima — Confessions of a Mask, Sun and Steel
  • Jean Genet — Our Lady of the Flowers
  • Clive Barker — sensual grotesque, transformation

Language as Violence

  • Antonin Artaud — Theatre of Cruelty
  • Clarice Lispector — The Passion According to G.H.
  • Samuel Beckett — The Unnamable
  • Dennis Cooper — George Miles cycle

Dread & Claustrophobia

  • Shirley Jackson — everyday spaces turning predatory
  • Franz Kafka — bureaucracy as nightmare
  • Thomas Ligotti — dread as philosophy
  • Angela Carter — gothic sensuality, mythic remix

Dream Logic

  • Jorge Luis Borges — labyrinths, doubles, reality as trapdoor
  • Maurice Blanchot — The Writing of the Disaster
  • Anne Carson — Decreation
